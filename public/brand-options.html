Fresh Feelarr marks

Option 1 and Option 4 stay in place. The eight new choices keep moving away from a standard app-tile formula, pulling from projector beams, editorial marks, cue cards, spools, prisms, constellation logic, slates, and folded glyphs.

Intent: preserve the strongest two directions, then widen the visual search with marks that feel media-aware without becoming Plex or Seerr copies.
Option 1

Stub

A ticket-like media artifact. It feels more cinema-native than app-native, with the clay punch acting as the request cue.

Option 2

Beam

A projector beam as discovery metaphor. It suggests finding the right thing to watch without drawing a literal screen.

Option 3

Latch

A chunky folded F-like mark. It is less cinema literal and more like a service symbol that could sit beside the Arr ecosystem.

Option 4

Ask

A redrawn prompt-bubble direction, warmer and bolder. It keeps the natural-language companion angle without matching the old sheet style.

Option 5

Cue

A title-card cue with a folded edge. It is media-forward, compact, and has enough asymmetry to avoid feeling generic.

Option 6

Spool

A reel reduced to a lollipop silhouette. More playful and analog, with the side band carrying the availability cue.

Option 7

Prism

A triangular split that feels like mood filtering. It is the most abstract, but it has a strong silhouette.

Option 8

Constellation

A recommendation graph made from three strong points. It implies “something like this” without showing media hardware.

Option 9

Slate

A clapperboard reduced to one block and one hinge. It is obviously media, but still avoids a playback triangle.

Option 10

Loom

A woven F/media glyph with a folded side. It is graphic, ownable, and less tied to any single integration.
